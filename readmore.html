🌸 My Story

👋 Hi, I’m Daniel-Ukpoju Ochanya Faith, You can call me Gentleme🌸, and I'm from Otukpo Local Government, Benue State.

🌟 I’m the last baby in of the house, and my siblings lovingly call me “Mummy’s Handbag” 💼💖.

🎓 Currently, I’m a Computer Science student at the University of Abuja. I’m super passionate about programming 💻✨ and love solving real-world problems with code.

🍲 Beyond coding, I absolutely love cooking — and one of my biggest dreams is to become a professional chef someday 👩‍🍳🌸. Cooking for me means spreading love, joy, and happiness through food ❤️.

🌍💚 My goal: Use technology, kindness, and creativity to make the world a better place 🌟.

But I’m not just about computers and code. I also dream of becoming a philanthropist and building a free healthcare center for my community. I want a world where everyone — rich or poor — can receive medical attention without worrying about money.

Growing up wasn’t easy. Life has been tough, but those challenges shaped me into who I am today. I know what it feels like to struggle, and that’s why I want children and the less privileged to know this truth: “You are not alone. Your voice matters. You can rise and be heard.”

With programming, I’ve already started solving problems. At my school, students often struggle to get access to learning materials. To help, I developed a Material Portal — a website where students can log in and download resources easily for free without paying a dime. It’s not live yet because I need funds to host it properly, but I’m determined to make it a reality soon.

This is just the beginning of my journey. I want to build, create, and inspire. Whether through code, food, or kindness, my goal is simple: to leave this world better than I found it. 🌍✨
